About the Calendar

This year's calendar is a celebration of the wonderful sites and views that we get to enjoy within our country. Distributed over 12 months, we hope that these images will bring out awe, excitement and curiosity in you to seek out the spectacular in life. We wish you an inspired year ahead.

January, 2023

Lake Nkunga - Meru

A panoramic crater Lake in Meru County located in the Eastern part of Kenya was revered and devotedly protected by locals. Considered sacred, the Lake was only accessible to rainmakers and elders who offered sacrifices to appease gods in times of calamity.

Namoratunga - Turkana

Namoratunga is a congregation of 19 pillars surrounded by a ring of over 20,000 stones located on the western side of Lake Turkana, about 50 M off the Lodwar-Kalokol road. Made of magnetic basalt, Namoratunga is as mysterious as it is ancient.

March, 2023

Kit Mikayi - Western Kenya

The legend of Kit Mikayi tells that many centuries ago lived an old man named Ngeso who was in great harmony with the stones. Every day when he woke up, he would enter the cave inside the stone and stay there all day, forcing his wife to bring him food in there.

April, 2023

Rock Gongs Of Lewa Downs

Lewa Downs lies on the northern slopes of Mt Kenya. The ancient rock gongs of Lewa Downs Wildlife Conservancy produce a range of sounds when struck with a hammer. It is believed that early man used these rock gongs for rituals and divining purposes.
